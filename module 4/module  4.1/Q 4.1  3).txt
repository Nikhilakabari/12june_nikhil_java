Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) is a programming that is based on the concept of "objects," which are instances of classes. OOP focuses on organizing code and data into reusable structures called classes, and it emphasizes the concept of encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism to model real-world entities and their relationships.
ex: c++,java

pop:Procedure Oriented Programming," which is a style of programming that focuses on procedures or functions to structure code. It's often contrasted with object-oriented programming (OOP). In POP, programs are typically organized around procedures or functions that perform specific tasks.
